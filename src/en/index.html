Manifesto

A humorous take on systems that refuse to work as intended. Each point is a valuable insight from an experienced expert, forming part of the philosophy of "Failing Architecture".

  • “It’ll do for now” — Eldar Musin
    Decisions are made quickly with minimal effort. As long as it "works," it's fine.
  • “Why is it so expensive?!” — Eldar Musin
    Quality and reliability are sacrificed in favor of cost savings.
  • “Why is it taking so long? We need it now” — Artyom Prima
    Speed trumps readiness, even if the system isn't launch-ready.
  • “We’ll rewrite it later” — Timur Zhamangarin
    Long-term planning is traded for quick results.
  • “Launched once. It crashed. You forgot” — Dmitry Remezov
    System stability is a secondary concern.
  • "Load balancing that balances only problems" — Saikhan Badmaev
    Errors are evenly distributed across all nodes.
  • "Monitoring and alerts that trigger only after a crash" — Saikhan Badmaev
    Issues are detected when it's already too late.
  • "Secrets and configurations only found in the afterlife" — Saikhan Badmaev
    Configurations are so complex that no one can locate them.
  • "Test environments that work only during tests" — Saikhan Badmaev
    Test environments are perfect, but production reveals surprises.
  • "The only expert is a gray-haired man with cognac and cigarettes" — Timur Akhmetov
    An indispensable figure without whom no one can maintain the system.
  • “We’ll move as is and fix it later” — Maxim Rudakov
    Migrations without preparation lead to new problems.
  • “All for the sake of MVP” — Saikhan Badmaev
    The minimum viable product justifies all decisions.
  • “Backups have never been needed. They won’t be needed now” — Saidazizkhon Akbarov
    Backups exist but are never tested.
  • “The system runs on Huanan hardware. Funny? No, it’s tragic” — Sam Bakh
    Using inappropriate hardware becomes the norm.
  • “We’ll just log it as tech debt” — Yaroslav Vengerskiy
    A convenient way to postpone solving problems indefinitely.
  • “It should’ve been built on another framework” — Eldos Matkerimov
    There's always a better idea after it's too late to change anything.
  • “Wit and bravery” — Bekarys Nurumbetov
    The perfect description of the team working with such architecture.
  • “Tests run as long as you don't run them” — Nurov Arnur
    Reliability is only tested in words.
  • “Safety comes last!” — Abdukhalikov Assir
    The best protection against DDOS attacks is a non-working product.
